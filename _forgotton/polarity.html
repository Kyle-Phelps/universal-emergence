I. What Is Polarity?

Polarity is not just positive and negative charge. It is directional desire—the tension between two paths, the motive force in a structured field.

Without polarity, there is no drive. No push or pull. No spin. No emergence.

II. Definition

Polarity (π̂) is defined as:

“Vector coupling per unit charge over angular momentum.”

It is how systems choose direction through desire differentials. It is not static—it shifts, flows, and phase-locks based on field context.

III. Polarity Behaviors

  • Protons (π̂⁺) push forward — directional impulse
  • Electrons (π̂⁻) stabilize from afar — coherence anchor
  • Neutrons (π̂⁰) wrap tension — passive terrain thickener

In humans, in thought, in systems—polarity is always present when something wants to become.
