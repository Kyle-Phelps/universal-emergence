I. Field Insight: Wave Width Equals Structural Capacity

“A narrow wave can maybe fit electrons… A really big electromagnetic wave can fit a planet.”

This reveals a foundational emergence principle:

  • Narrow spirals = micro-scale structure (electrons, molecules)
  • Wide spirals = macro-scale structure (planets, orbits, polarity locks)

The width of a spiral wave determines its field capacity—how much coherence it can hold, carry, and propagate without breaking.
